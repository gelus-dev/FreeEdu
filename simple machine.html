Simple Machine

Learn Simple Machine concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview – Simple & Advanced Simple Machines

Definition: A simple machine is a device that changes the direction or magnitude of a force to make work easier. Advanced simple machines consider factors like compound systems, friction, efficiency, and load distribution.

Types of Simple Machines:

  • Lever: Rigid bar pivoted on a fulcrum. Classes: First, Second, Third.
  • Pulley: Wheel and rope to lift/load. Fixed, Movable, Block & Tackle.
  • Wheel & Axle: Rotating wheel to amplify force or speed.
  • Inclined Plane: Slanted surface reduces effort over distance.
  • Wedge: Two inclined planes back-to-back.
  • Screw: Inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder.

Advanced Concepts:

  • Compound Machines: Combination of two or more simple machines.
  • Mechanical Advantage (MA): Ratio of load to effort. Multiply individual MAs for compound machines.
  • Velocity Ratio (VR): Distance moved by effort / distance moved by load. Multiply for compound machines.
  • Efficiency (η): η = (MA / VR) × 100%. Friction reduces efficiency.
  • Frictional Effects: Real machines have friction, which increases effort and reduces efficiency.

Examples – Simple & Advanced

Example 1: Lever

Given: Load = 200 N, Effort = 50 N, Effort arm = 0.5 m
Find: Load arm
Solution: MA = Load / Effort = 200/50 = 4
Load arm = Effort arm × MA = 0.5 × 4 = 2 m

Example 2: Pulley System

Given: Load = 300 N, Effort = 100 N
Find: MA
Solution: MA = Load / Effort = 300 / 100 = 3

Example 3: Inclined Plane

Given: Load = 500 N, Effort = 100 N
Find: MA
Solution: MA = Load / Effort = 500 / 100 = 5

Example 4: Compound Lever & Pulley

Given: Lever MA = 4, Pulley MA = 3, Load = 360 N
Find: Total effort required
Solution: Total MA = 4 × 3 = 12
Effort = 360 / 12 = 30 N

Example 5: Efficiency of Pulley System

Given: Load = 500 N, Effort = 120 N, VR = 5
Find: Efficiency
Solution: MA = Load / Effort = 500 / 120 ≈ 4.167
Efficiency = (MA / VR) × 100 ≈ 83.34%

Example 6: Screw Jack

Given: Screw radius = 0.025 m, pitch = 0.005 m, Load = 1000 N, efficiency = 60%
Find: Effort required
Solution: Ideal MA = 2πr / pitch = 2π*0.025 / 0.005 ≈ 31.416
Effective MA = 0.6 × 31.416 ≈ 18.85
Effort = 1000 / 18.85 ≈ 53.07 N
