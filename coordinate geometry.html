Coordinate geometry

Learn about points, lines, circles, conics, formulas, and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Coordinate Geometry (Analytic Geometry) studies geometric figures using a coordinate system — typically the Cartesian plane — by representing points as ordered pairs (x, y) and expressing curves and lines with algebraic equations.

Key Concepts:

Basic Concepts & Formulas:

• Distance Formula: Distance between P(x₁,y₁) and Q(x₂,y₂): d = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²].
• Midpoint Formula: Midpoint of PQ: ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2).
• Section Formula (Internal, ratio m:n): Point dividing P(x₁,y₁) and Q(x₂,y₂) internally in ratio m:n is ((m x₂ + n x₁)/(m+n), (m y₂ + n y₁)/(m+n)).
• Slope (Gradient): Slope of line through P and Q: m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁) (vertical line: undefined).
• Angle between lines with slopes m₁ and m₂: tan θ = |(m₂ − m₁)/(1 + m₁ m₂)|.
• Condition for parallel/perpendicular: Parallel ⇔ m₁ = m₂. Perpendicular ⇔ m₁·m₂ = −1.
• Equation of a line: Point–slope: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁). Two-point: (y−y₁)/(y₂−y₁) = (x−x₁)/(x₂−x₁). Slope-intercept: y = mx + c. General: Ax + By + C = 0.
• Area of triangle (coordinates): For (x₁,y₁),(x₂,y₂),(x₃,y₃): Area = 1/2 | x₁(y₂−y₃)+x₂(y₃−y₁)+x₃(y₁−y₂) |.
• Collinearity: Three points are collinear ⇔ area (above determinant) = 0 (or slopes equal).
• Circle (standard): Center (h,k), radius r: (x−h)² + (y−k)² = r².
• Conic sections (standard forms): Parabola: y² = 4ax (or rotated forms). Ellipse: x²/a² + y²/b² = 1. Hyperbola: x²/a² − y²/b² = 1.
• Locus: Set of points satisfying a given geometric condition expressed algebraically (e.g., locus of points equidistant from a point and a line is a parabola).

Advanced Tools / Tricks:

• Use vector form and dot/cross products for projection, perpendicularity, angles, and area.
• Use coordinate transformations (translation/rotation) to simplify conics or eliminate cross-terms.
• For many contest problems, place convenient coordinates (origin, axes along sides) to simplify calculations.
• Use symmetry and parametric forms (x = a cos t, y = b sin t for ellipse) when helpful.

Examples

Example 1: Distance Between Two Points

Given: P(3, 4) and Q(−1, 2).
Find: Distance PQ.
Solution: d = √[(−1−3)² + (2−4)²] = √[(−4)² + (−2)²] = √(16 + 4) = √20 = 2√5.
Explanation: Applied the distance formula directly.

Example 2: Midpoint & Section Formula

Given: A(1, 2) and B(5, 8).
Find: Midpoint of AB and the point dividing AB internally in ratio 1:2 (A to B is 1:2).
Solution: Midpoint = ((1+5)/2, (2+8)/2) = (3, 5).
Point in ratio 1:2 (m:n with m=1 at B, n=2 at A by formula convention) = ((1·5 + 2·1)/(1+2), (1·8 + 2·2)/(1+2)) = ( (5+2)/3, (8+4)/3 ) = (7/3, 12/3) = (7/3, 4).
Explanation: Used midpoint formula and internal section formula.

Example 3: Equation of Line through Two Points

Given: Points (2, 3) and (5, −3).
Find: Equation of the line in slope-intercept form.
Solution: Slope m = (−3 − 3)/(5 − 2) = −6/3 = −2. Use point-slope with (2,3): y − 3 = −2(x − 2) ⇒ y − 3 = −2x + 4 ⇒ y = −2x + 7.
Explanation: Compute slope then use point-slope form.

Example 4: Area of a Triangle Using Coordinates

Given: Vertices (0,0), (4,0), (0,3).
Find: Area.
Solution: Area = 1/2 |0(0−3) + 4(3−0) + 0(0−0)| = 1/2 |0 + 12 + 0| = 6.
Explanation: Determinant formula simplifies to 1/2 · base · height for right triangle.

Example 5: Equation of a Circle

Given: Centre C(2, −1) and radius r = 3.
Find: Equation of the circle.
Solution: (x − 2)² + (y + 1)² = 9.
Explanation: Standard circle equation (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r².

Example 6: Collinearity Test

Given: Points (1,2), (2,4), (3,6).
Find: Are they collinear?
Solution: Slope between first two = (4−2)/(2−1) = 2. Slope between last two = (6−4)/(3−2) = 2. Slopes equal ⇒ points are collinear.
Explanation: Equal slopes (or zero area via determinant) imply collinearity.

Answer Key

Answer 1:

Answer: b) 2√5.
Explanation: d = √[(−1−3)² + (2−4)²] = √(16+4) = √20 = 2√5.

Answer 2:

Answer: b) (3, 5).
Explanation: Midpoint = ((1+5)/2, (2+8)/2) = (3,5).

Answer 3:

Answer: b) −2.
Explanation: m = (−3−3)/(5−2) = −6/3 = −2.

Answer 4:

Answer: b) y = −1/2 x + 1.
Explanation: Lines perpendicular to y = 2x + 3 must have slope −1/2.

Answer 5:

Answer: b) (x − 2)² + (y + 1)² = 9.
Explanation: Standard form (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r² with (h,k) = (2,−1), r = 3.

Answer 6:

Answer: b) 6.
Explanation: Right triangle with base = 4 and height = 3 ⇒ Area = 1/2 · 4 · 3 = 6.

Answer 7:

Answer: a) (4, 1).
Explanation: For points (2,−1) and (8,5) divided internally in ratio 1:2 (from first to second): x = (1·8 + 2·2)/3 = (8+4)/3 = 4, y = (1·5 + 2·(−1))/3 = (5−2)/3 = 1.

Answer 8:

Answer: a) Yes.
Explanation: Slope (1st,2nd) = (4−2)/(2−1) = 2; slope (2nd,3rd) = (6−4)/(3−2) = 2 ⇒ all three have same slope ⇒ collinear.

Answer 9:

Answer: a) √5/3.
Explanation: For ellipse x²/a² + y²/b² = 1 with a² = 9, b² = 4 (a > b). e = √(1 − b²/a²) = √(1 − 4/9) = √(5/9) = √5/3.

Answer 10:

Answer: a) y = −2x + 8.
Explanation: Midpoint of (1,1) and (5,3) is (3,2). Slope of segment = (3−1)/(5−1) = 2/4 = 1/2. Perpendicular slope = −2. Equation: y−2 = −2(x−3) ⇒ y = −2x + 8.
