Animal Classification

Learn Animal Classification and Practice Questions✅

Overview

Definition: Animal Classification is the scientific arrangement of animals into groups based on similarities and differences in their body structure, function, and evolutionary history.

Levels of Classification:

1. Kingdom Animalia: Multicellular, eukaryotic, heterotrophic organisms.

2. Phylum: Divides animals based on major body plans.

3. Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species: Further divisions based on structural and functional characteristics.

Major Phyla (Secondary & High School Level):

1. Porifera (Sponges): Pores, no tissues, e.g., Sycon.

2. Cnidaria: Stinging cells, radial symmetry, e.g., Hydra, Jellyfish.

3. Platyhelminthes: Flatworms, bilateral symmetry, e.g., Planaria.

4. Nematoda: Roundworms, pseudocoelomate, e.g., Ascaris.

5. Annelida: Segmented worms, true coelom, e.g., Earthworm.

6. Arthropoda: Jointed legs, exoskeleton, largest phylum, e.g., Insects, Crabs.

7. Mollusca: Soft body, shell, e.g., Octopus, Snail.

8. Echinodermata: Spiny skin, radial symmetry in adults, e.g., Starfish.

9. Chordata: Notochord, dorsal nerve cord, e.g., Fish, Amphibians, Reptiles, Birds, Mammals.

Important Secondary & High School Notes:

• Vertebrates vs Invertebrates: Presence or absence of backbone.
• Cold-blooded vs Warm-blooded: Regulation of body temperature.
• Examples of Vertebrate Classes: Pisces (fish), Amphibia, Reptilia, Aves (birds), Mammalia.
