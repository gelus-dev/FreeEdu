Articles

Learn Articles and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Articles are words that define a noun as specific or unspecific. In English, there are three articles: a, an, and the.

Key Concepts :

1. Types of Articles:
• Indefinite Articles – a, an
• Definite Article – the

2. Usage of 'a':
• Used before singular countable nouns starting with a consonant sound
• Example: a boy, a dog, a university (u = consonant sound)

3. Usage of 'an':
• Used before singular countable nouns starting with a vowel sound
• Example: an apple, an hour (h is silent, so vowel sound)

4. Usage of 'the':
• Used before specific nouns already known to the listener/reader
• Used with unique things (the sun, the moon)
• Used with superlatives (the best, the tallest)
• Used with holy books, rivers, mountains, seas, newspapers

5. Omission of Articles:
• No article is used before proper nouns (e.g., Mount Everest, Ram)
• No article before abstract nouns used in general sense (e.g., honesty is the best policy)
• No article before plural nouns used in general sense (e.g., Dogs are loyal)
