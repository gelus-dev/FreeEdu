Carbon and Its Compounds

Learn Carbon and Its Compounds and examples ✅

Overview

Atomic Number: 6

Electronic Configuration: 2, 4

Valency: 4 (tetravalent)

Position: Group 14, Period 2 of periodic table

Symbol: C

Unique Properties of Carbon

Catenation: Forms long chains due to strong C-C bonds (348 kJ/mol), can form straight chains, branched chains, rings

Tetravalency: Forms 4 covalent bonds; sp³, sp², sp hybridization

Small Size: Forms strong covalent bonds, allows multiple bonds (double/triple bonds)

Allotropes of Carbon

Crystalline:

  • Diamond: Each C bonded to 4 others, sp³, 3D network, hardest substance, insulator
  • Graphite: Each C bonded to 3 others, sp², layered hexagonal, soft, good conductor
  • Fullerenes: C₆₀, football-like structure, nanotechnology

Amorphous: Coal, Charcoal, Coke, Carbon black

Hydrocarbons

Alkanes (Saturated): CₙH₂ₙ₊₂, CH₄, C₂H₆, single bonds, suffix -ane

Alkenes (Unsaturated): CₙH₂ₙ, CH₂=CH₂, double bond, suffix -ene

Alkynes (Unsaturated): CₙH₂ₙ₋₂, C₂H₂, triple bond, suffix -yne

Functional Groups

  • Alcohols: -OH, e.g., Ethanol, suffix -ol
  • Aldehydes: -CHO, e.g., Formaldehyde, suffix -al
  • Ketones: >C=O, e.g., Acetone, suffix -one
  • Carboxylic Acids: -COOH, e.g., Acetic acid, suffix -oic acid
  • Esters: -COO-, e.g., Ethyl acetate, suffix -oate

Homologous Series

Compounds with same functional group, successive members differ by CH₂, similar chemical properties

Nomenclature (IUPAC Rules)

  1. Find longest carbon chain
  2. Number carbons from end nearest functional group
  3. Identify and number substituents
  4. Name: substituents + root + suffix

Key Formulas & Reactions

Combustion: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O; C₂H₄ + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 2H₂O

Addition Reactions: C₂H₄ + H₂ → C₂H₆; C₂H₄ + HCl → C₂H₅Cl

Oxidation: C₂H₅OH + O₂ → CH₃COOH + H₂O

Fermentation: C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂
