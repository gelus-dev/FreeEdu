Chemical Reaction

Learn Chemical Reaction and examples ✅

Overview

Definition: A Chemical Reaction is a process in which one or more substances (reactants) are transformed into new substances (products) with different chemical properties. It involves breaking of old bonds and formation of new bonds.

Types of Chemical Reactions:

1. Combination Reaction: Two or more substances combine to form a single product.
Example: \( A + B \rightarrow AB \)

2. Decomposition Reaction: A single compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances.
Example: \( AB \rightarrow A + B \)

3. Displacement Reaction: An element displaces another element from a compound.
Example: \( A + BC \rightarrow AC + B \)

4. Double Displacement Reaction: Exchange of ions between two compounds.
Example: \( AB + CD \rightarrow AD + CB \)

5. Redox Reaction: Involves transfer of electrons; includes oxidation and reduction.
Example: \( Zn + Cu^{2+} \rightarrow Zn^{2+} + Cu \)

6. Acid-Base Reaction: Neutralization between acid and base.
Example: \( HCl + NaOH \rightarrow NaCl + H_2O \)

7. Precipitation Reaction: Formation of insoluble product from two solutions.
Example: \( AgNO_3 + NaCl \rightarrow AgCl \downarrow + NaNO_3 \)

Important Formulas / Concepts (Secondary & High School Level):

• Mole concept: \( n = \frac{m}{M} \), \( n = \frac{C \cdot V}{1000} \)
• Stoichiometry: \( aA + bB \rightarrow cC + dD \) (moles ratio \( a:b:c:d \))
• Gas laws: \( PV = nRT \), \( P_1V_1/T_1 = P_2V_2/T_2 \)
• Concentration: \( C = \frac{n}{V} \)
• Oxidation number rules for redox reactions
• Energy change: \( \Delta H = \sum H_{\rm bonds\ broken} - \sum H_{\rm bonds\ formed} \)

Examples

Example 1: Combination Reaction

Given: \( 2H_2 + O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2O \)
Type: Combination
Solution: Two reactants form a single product.

Example 2: Decomposition Reaction

Given: \( 2KClO_3 \rightarrow 2KCl + 3O_2 \)
Type: Decomposition
Solution: One compound breaks into two products.

Example 3: Displacement Reaction

Given: \( Zn + CuSO_4 \rightarrow ZnSO_4 + Cu \)
Type: Single displacement
Solution: Zinc displaces copper from copper sulfate.

Example 4: Double Displacement Reaction

Given: \( Na_2SO_4 + BaCl_2 \rightarrow BaSO_4 \downarrow + 2NaCl \)
Type: Double displacement
Solution: Exchange of ions forms precipitate BaSO4.

Example 5: Redox Reaction

Given: \( 2Fe + 3Cl_2 \rightarrow 2FeCl_3 \)
Solution: Iron is oxidized (0 → +3), chlorine is reduced (0 → -1).
