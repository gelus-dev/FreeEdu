Indices

Learn about Indices concepts, operations and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Indices (or exponents) represent how many times a number (base) is multiplied by itself. For example, \( 2^3 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8 \).

Basic Laws of Indices:

• \( a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n} \)
• \( \frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n} \)
• \( (a^m)^n = a^{mn} \)
• \( (ab)^n = a^n b^n \)
• \( \frac{a^m}{b^m} = \left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^m \)
• \( a^0 = 1 \) (for \( a \neq 0 \))
• \( a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n} \)

Advanced Concepts:

• Fractional indices: \( a^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{a^m} \)
• Negative fractional indices: \( a^{-m/n} = \frac{1}{\sqrt[n]{a^m}} \)
• Solving exponential equations using laws of indices.
• Simplifying expressions with multiple bases and exponents.

Examples

Example 1: Basic Multiplication

Given: \( 2^3 \cdot 2^4 \)
Solution: \( 2^{3+4} = 2^7 = 128 \)

Example 2: Division

Given: \( \frac{5^7}{5^3} \)
Solution: \( 5^{7-3} = 5^4 = 625 \)

Example 3: Power of a Power

Given: \( (3^2)^4 \)
Solution: \( 3^{2*4} = 3^8 = 6561 \)

Example 4: Negative Index

Given: \( 2^{-3} \)
Solution: \( \frac{1}{2^3} = \frac{1}{8} \)

Example 5: Fractional Index

Given: \( 8^{2/3} \)
Solution: \( \sqrt[3]{8^2} = \sqrt[3]{64} = 4 \)

Example 6: Combining Laws

Given: \( \frac{2^3 \cdot 4^2}{8} \)
Solution: Rewrite 4 and 8 as powers of 2: \( \frac{2^3 \cdot 2^4}{2^3} = 2^4 = 16 \)
