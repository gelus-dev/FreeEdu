Reported Speech

Learn Reported Speech and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Reported Speech, also called indirect speech, is the way of reporting what someone else has said without quoting their exact words.

Key Concepts :

1. Direct vs Indirect Speech:
• Direct: She said, "I am reading a book."
• Indirect: She said that she was reading a book.

2. Changes in Reported Speech:
• **Tense:** Usually shifts back one tense in past reporting.
- Present → Past: "I am happy" → He said that he was happy.
- Present Perfect → Past Perfect: "I have finished" → He said that he had finished.
- Past → Past Perfect: "I went there" → He said that he had gone there.
- Future → Conditional: "I will come" → He said that he would come.
• **Pronouns:** Change according to context.
- "I" → "he/she", "my" → "his/her"
• **Time expressions:** Adjust according to reporting time.
- today → that day, tomorrow → the next day, yesterday → the day before

3. Reporting Verbs:
• Common verbs: said, told, asked, requested, ordered, advised
• "Told" requires an object: He told me that…
• "Said" can be used without an object: He said that…

4. Questions in Reported Speech:
• Yes/No questions: use 'if/whether'
- Direct: "Are you coming?" → Indirect: He asked if I was coming.
• Wh-questions: keep the question word
- Direct: "Where do you live?" → Indirect: He asked where I lived.

5. Commands & Requests:
• Positive command: "Close the door" → He asked me to close the door.
• Negative command: "Don’t touch it" → He told me not to touch it.
• Requests: "Please help me" → He requested me to help him.

6. Exceptions & Special Cases:
• If reporting verb is in present, tense change is not necessary.
- Direct: "I am tired," he says → Indirect: He says that he is tired.
• Modal verbs 'can', 'may', 'must' change in past:
- can → could, may → might, must → had to
• Some words remain unchanged: should, might, could, would

Question 1:

B) She said that she was reading a book.
C) She said that she had read a book.
D) She said that she is reading a book.
