Learn key concepts of matrices and determinants with solved examples and questions ✅

Overview

Definition: A matrix is a rectangular array of numbers arranged in rows and columns. A determinant is a scalar associated with a square matrix, useful in solving linear equations and finding inverses.

Types of Matrices:

1. Row Matrix: Single row, e.g., \( [1,2,3] \)

2. Column Matrix: Single column, e.g., \( \begin{bmatrix}1\\2\\3\end{bmatrix} \)

3. Square Matrix: Rows = Columns, e.g., \( \begin{bmatrix}1 & 2\\3 & 4\end{bmatrix} \)

4. Zero Matrix: All elements 0, e.g., \( \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0\\0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \)

5. Identity Matrix: Diagonal 1s, others 0, e.g., \( I_3 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0\\0 & 1 & 0\\0 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \)

Matrix Operations:

• Addition/Subtraction: Element-wise
• Scalar Multiplication: Multiply each element by a scalar
• Matrix Multiplication: \( (AB)_{ij} = \sum_k A_{ik} B_{kj} \)

Determinant Formulas:

• 2×2: \( \det\begin{bmatrix}a & b\\c & d\end{bmatrix} = ad - bc \)
• 3×3: \( \det\begin{bmatrix}a & b & c\\d & e & f\\g & h & i\end{bmatrix} = a(ei - fh) - b(di - fg) + c(dh - eg) \)

Examples

Example 1: Matrix Addition

Given: \( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 2\\3 & 4\end{bmatrix}, B = \begin{bmatrix}5 & 6\\7 & 8\end{bmatrix} \)
Find: \( A + B \)
Solution: \( A+B = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 8\\10 & 12\end{bmatrix} \)

Example 2: Matrix Multiplication

Given: \( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 2\\3 & 4\end{bmatrix}, B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 0\\1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \)
Find: \( AB \)
Solution: \( AB = \begin{bmatrix}4 & 6\\10 & 12\end{bmatrix} \)

Example 3: Determinant of 2×2

Given: \( A = \begin{bmatrix}3 & 5\\2 & 7\end{bmatrix} \)
Solution: \( \det(A) = 3*7 - 5*2 = 11 \)

Example 4: Determinant of 3×3

Given: \( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & 3\\4 & 5 & 6\\7 & 8 & 9\end{bmatrix} \)
Solution: \( \det(A) = 0 \)
