Causative Verbs

Learn Causative Verbs and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Causative Verbs are verbs that show someone causing another person to do something. Common causative verbs in English are make, have, get, and let.

Key Concepts :

1. Make:
• Structure: Subject + make + object + verb (base form)
• Meaning: To force or compel someone to do something
• Example meaning: The teacher makes students study.

2. Have:
• Structure: Subject + have + object + verb (base form)
• Meaning: To give responsibility/order to someone
• Example meaning: I had my assistant send the email.

3. Get:
• Structure: Subject + get + object + to + verb
• Meaning: To persuade/convince someone to do something
• Example meaning: She got her brother to fix the computer.

4. Let:
• Structure: Subject + let + object + verb (base form)
• Meaning: To allow or permit someone to do something
• Example meaning: His parents let him play outside.

5. Have/Get Something Done (Causative Passive):
• Structure: Subject + have/get + object + past participle
• Meaning: To arrange for someone else to do something for you
• Example meaning: I had my hair cut yesterday.
