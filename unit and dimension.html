Unit and Dimension

Learn Unit and Dimension concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Unit is a standard quantity used to measure physical quantities. Dimension represents the physical nature of a quantity in terms of basic quantities like Mass (M), Length (L), and Time (T).

Fundamental Quantities & Units:

• Mass (M) – kilogram (kg)
• Length (L) – meter (m)
• Time (T) – second (s)
• Electric current (I) – ampere (A)
• Temperature (Θ) – kelvin (K)
• Luminous intensity (J) – candela (cd)
• Amount of substance (N) – mole (mol)

Derived Quantities & Dimensions:

• Velocity: \( v = \frac{L}{T} \) → [L T^-1]
• Acceleration: \( a = \frac{L}{T^2} \) → [L T^-2]
• Force: \( F = m a \) → [M L T^-2]
• Energy/Work: \( E = F \cdot L \) → [M L^2 T^-2]
• Power: \( P = \frac{E}{T} \) → [M L^2 T^-3]
• Pressure: \( P = \frac{F}{L^2} \) → [M L^-1 T^-2]
• Density: \( \rho = \frac{M}{L^3} \) → [M L^-3]
• Surface Tension: [M T^-2]
• Viscosity: [M L^-1 T^-1]
• Angular Momentum: [M L^2 T^-1]
• Torque: [M L^2 T^-2]

Principle of Homogeneity:

• In any physically meaningful equation, the dimensions on both sides must be identical.
• Example: \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)
LHS: [L], RHS: ut → [L], at^2 → [L], dimensionally correct.

Tips for Competitive Exams:

  • Check dimensions before solving formula-based problems.
  • Use dimensional analysis to find formulas (e.g., period of a pendulum, speed of wave).
  • Trick questions often involve incorrect powers or missing constants.
  • Remember derived quantities involving multiple fundamentals (e.g., angular quantities).

Examples

Example 1: Dimensional Formula of Force

Given: Force \( F = ma \)
Solution: Mass [M], Acceleration [L T^-2] → Force = [M L T^-2]

Example 2: Dimensional Formula of Pressure

Given: Pressure \( P = \frac{F}{A} \)
Solution: Force [M L T^-2], Area [L^2] → Pressure = [M L^-1 T^-2]

Example 3: Dimensional Consistency Check

Equation: \( v = u + at \)
Solution: v, u → [L T^-1], a t → [L T^-2][T] = [L T^-1], Correct.

Example 4: Finding Formula Using Dimensions

Problem: Period T of a simple pendulum depends on length l and gravity g.
Assume \( T \propto l^a g^b \)
[T] = [L]^a [L T^-2]^b → [T] = [L]^{a+b}[T]^{-2b}
Equate powers: -2b = 1 → b = -1/2, a+b = 0 → a = 1/2
Solution: \( T \propto \sqrt{\frac{l}{g}} \)
