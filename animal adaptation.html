Animal Adaptation

Learn Animal Adaptation and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Animal Adaptation is the process by which animals develop special features (structural, behavioral, or physiological) that help them survive, reproduce, and thrive in their environment.

Types of Adaptations (School Level Concepts):

1. Structural Adaptation: Physical features that help survival. • Thick fur in polar bears for cold. • Webbed feet in ducks for swimming.

2. Behavioral Adaptation: Changes in behavior to survive. • Migration of birds. • Hibernation of bears in winter.

3. Physiological Adaptation: Internal body processes for survival. • Snakes producing venom. • Desert animals conserving water.

Secondary & High School Important Notes:

• Desert Adaptations: Camels store fat in humps, long eyelashes protect eyes from sand. • Aquatic Adaptations: Fishes have gills, streamlined bodies. • Cold Climate Adaptations: Polar bears with blubber, penguins huddle for warmth. • Defense Adaptations: Chameleons change color, porcupines have spines. • Special Adaptations: Nocturnal animals (bats, owls) active at night; mimicry and camouflage for protection.
