Metals and Non-metals

Learn Metals and Non-metals and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Metals are elements that are generally hard, malleable, ductile, good conductors of heat and electricity, and tend to lose electrons to form positive ions. Non-metals are elements that are usually soft or brittle, poor conductors, and tend to gain electrons to form negative ions.

General Properties:

Metals: Lustrous, high melting & boiling points, malleable, ductile, conductors, form basic oxides.

Non-Metals: Dull appearance, low melting & boiling points, brittle (solid), poor conductors, form acidic oxides.

Examples:

Metals: Na, K, Mg, Fe, Cu, Al

Non-Metals: H, C, N, O, S, Cl

Metallurgy (Secondary & High School Level):

Steps of Metallurgy:

  1. Concentration of ore: Removal of impurities by methods like gravity separation, magnetic separation, froth flotation.
  2. Calcination: Heating of ore in absence of air to remove volatile impurities.
  3. Roasting: Heating in presence of oxygen to convert sulfides to oxides.
  4. Reduction: Converting metal oxides to metal using reducing agents like carbon, CO, or electrolysis.
  5. Refining: Purification of metals by methods like electrolytic refining, distillation, zone refining.

Electrolytic Refining:

Impure metal is made the anode, pure metal forms the cathode. Electrolyte contains metal ions. On passing current, pure metal deposits on cathode.

Formulas / Reactions:

• Metal Oxide + Acid → Salt + Water
• Metal + Oxygen → Metal Oxide
• Metal + Halogen → Metal Halide
• Electrolytic Refining: \( \text{Anode: impure Cu → Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻} \), \( \text{Cathode: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu (pure)} \)

Examples

Example 1: Reaction of Metal with Acid

Given: Zn + H₂SO₄
Solution: \( \text{Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂} \)

Example 2: Electrolytic Refining of Copper

Given: Impure Cu anode in CuSO₄ solution
Solution: Cu²⁺ ions move to cathode forming pure Cu, impurities settle as anode mud.

Example 3: Identifying Metal & Non-Metal

Given: Mg and S
Solution: Mg is lustrous, malleable, conductor → Metal; S is brittle, dull, non-conductor → Non-metal.

Example 4: Reduction of Metal Oxide

Given: Fe₂O₃ with CO
Solution: \( \text{Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂} \)
