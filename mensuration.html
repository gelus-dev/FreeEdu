Mensuration

Learn about Mensuration concepts, operations and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Mensuration is the branch of mathematics that deals with measuring geometric figures, including lengths, areas, and volumes.

Basic Formulas:

1. Perimeter / Circumference: Circle: C = 2πr, Square: P = 4a, Rectangle: P = 2(l+b)

2. Area: Rectangle: A = l × b, Square: A = a², Triangle: A = ½ × base × height, Circle: A = πr², Trapezium: A = ½ × (sum of parallel sides) × height

3. Surface Area (SA): Cube: SA = 6a², Cuboid: SA = 2(lb + bh + hl), Sphere: SA = 4πr², Cylinder: SA = 2πr(h+r), Cone: SA = πr(l+r)

4. Volume (V): Cube: V = a³, Cuboid: V = l × b × h, Sphere: V = 4/3 πr³, Cylinder: V = πr²h, Cone: V = 1/3 πr²h

Examples

Example 1: Area of Rectangle

Length: 8 cm, Breadth: 5 cm
Solution: A = l × b = 8 × 5 = 40 cm²

Example 2: Circumference of Circle

Radius: 7 cm
Solution: C = 2πr = 2 × 22/7 × 7 = 44 cm

Example 3: Volume of Cylinder

Radius: 7 cm, Height: 10 cm
Solution: V = πr²h = 22/7 × 49 × 10 = 1540 cm³
