Materials Used In Daily Life

Learn Materials Used In Daily Life and examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Materials used in daily life refer to natural and synthetic substances that are applied in household, industrial, and technological purposes. Their selection depends on properties like strength, conductivity, flexibility, durability, and chemical reactivity.

Classification of Materials:

1. Metals: Good conductors of heat and electricity, malleable, ductile, e.g., iron, copper, aluminum.

2. Non-metals: Poor conductors, brittle in solid state, e.g., sulfur, phosphorus, carbon.

3. Alloys: Mixtures of metals to improve properties, e.g., steel (iron + carbon), brass (copper + zinc).

4. Polymers/Plastics: Long-chain molecules, flexible, low density, e.g., polyethylene, PVC, nylon.

5. Ceramics and Glass: Hard, brittle, heat-resistant, e.g., porcelain, window glass.

6. Composites: Combination of two or more materials to improve strength and reduce weight, e.g., fiberglass, plywood.

7. Soaps and Detergents: Cleaning agents. Soaps are sodium or potassium salts of fatty acids, biodegradable. Detergents are synthetic surfactants, effective in hard water.

Important Properties:

  • Hardness and strength
  • Conductivity (electrical and thermal)
  • Elasticity and plasticity
  • Corrosion resistance
  • Density and melting point
  • Cleaning efficiency (for soaps and detergents)

Daily Life Examples:

Metals: Cooking utensils (aluminum), wiring (copper), nails (iron)

Non-metals: Matches (sulfur), pencils (graphite), fertilizers (phosphorus)

Alloys: Coins (cupronickel), machinery (steel)

Polymers: Water bottles (PET), pipes (PVC), clothes (nylon)

Ceramics/Glass: Crockery, windows, tiles

Composites: Sports equipment, aerospace components

Soaps: Cleaning hands, laundry (sodium stearate, potassium stearate)

Detergents: Washing clothes, dishes, synthetic surfactants

Examples

Example 1: Identify Materials

Question: Classify the following into metals, non-metals, alloys, polymers, or cleaning agents: Copper, Sulfur, Brass, PVC, Soap.
Solution: Copper → Metal, Sulfur → Non-metal, Brass → Alloy, PVC → Polymer, Soap → Cleaning agent

Example 2: Property Application

Question: Which material would you choose for washing clothes effectively in hard water?
Solution: Detergent because it works even in hard water, unlike soap which forms scum.

Example 3: Daily Use Scenario

Question: Name a polymer used for water bottles.
Solution: Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET)

Example 4: Composite Material

Question: Identify a composite used in sports equipment.
Solution: Fiberglass (glass fibers + polymer matrix)
