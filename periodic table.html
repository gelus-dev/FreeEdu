Periodic Table

Learn Periodic Table and examples ✅

Overview

Definition: The Periodic Table is a tabular arrangement of chemical elements in order of increasing atomic number, showing periodic recurrence of chemical properties. Elements in the same group have similar properties due to the same number of valence electrons.

Structure of Periodic Table:

1. Periods: Horizontal rows (1–7), properties vary across a period.
2. Groups/Families: Vertical columns (1–18), elements have similar chemical behavior.
3. Blocks: s-block, p-block, d-block, f-block based on electron configuration.
4. Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons in the nucleus.
5. Atomic Mass (A): Average mass of an element's isotopes.

Important Periodic Properties:

• Atomic radius (r): Decreases across a period, increases down a group.
• Ionization energy (IE): Increases across a period, decreases down a group.
• Electron affinity (EA): Becomes more negative across a period.
• Electronegativity (χ): Increases across a period, decreases down a group.
• Metallic character: Decreases across a period, increases down a group.

Key Formulas/Relations:

• Effective nuclear charge: \( Z_{\rm eff} = Z - S \), where \(S\) is shielding constant.
• Ionic radius relation: \( r_{\rm cation} < r_{\rm atom} < r_{\rm anion} \)
• Ionization energy trend: \( IE \propto \frac{Z_{\rm eff}}{n^2} \), where \( n \) is principal quantum number.
• Electronegativity approximate (Pauling scale): \( \chi \propto \frac{IE + EA}{2} \)

Examples

Example 1: Identifying Group and Period

Given: Element with atomic number 17.
Find: Group and period.
Solution: Z=17 → Chlorine (Cl), Period 3, Group 17 (halogen)

Example 2: Trend in Atomic Radius

Compare: Na, Mg, Al
Solution: Atomic radius decreases across period: \( r_{\rm Na} > r_{\rm Mg} > r_{\rm Al} \)

Example 3: Ionization Energy

Given: Li, Be, B
Solution: IE increases across period: \( IE_{\rm Li} < IE_{\rm Be} < IE_{\rm B} \)

Example 4: Electronegativity Comparison

Given: F, Cl, Br
Solution: χ decreases down group: \( \chi_{\rm F} > \chi_{\rm Cl} > \chi_{\rm Br} \)
