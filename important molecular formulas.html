Important Molecular Formulas

Learn Important Molecular Formulas and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Chemicals used in daily life include substances with specific chemical formulas and properties used in household, industrial, and personal applications. Understanding their chemical composition is important at secondary and high school levels.

Important Chemicals & Formulas:

1. Water: \( H_2O \) – universal solvent, used in drinking, cooking, cleaning.

2. Sodium Chloride: \( NaCl \) – table salt, used in food preservation and cooking.

3. Baking Soda: \( NaHCO_3 \) – used in baking, cleaning, and neutralizing acids.

4. Sulfuric Acid: \( H_2SO_4 \) – used in batteries, fertilizers, and industrial processes.

5. Hydrochloric Acid: \( HCl \) – used in cleaning, stomach acid, and chemical reactions.

6. Ammonia: \( NH_3 \) – used in cleaning agents, fertilizers.

7. Lime: \( CaO \) – used in construction and water treatment.

8. Soap: Sodium or potassium salts of fatty acids (e.g., \( C_{17}H_{35}COONa \)) – cleansing agent.

9. Detergent: Synthetic surfactants (e.g., \( C_{12}H_{25}SO_4Na \)) – effective cleaning in hard water.

10. Ethanol: \( C_2H_5OH \) – used in beverages, disinfectants, and solvents.

Properties & Uses:

  • Acids: Corrosive, sour taste, pH < 7
  • Bases/Alkalis: Bitter, slippery, pH > 7
  • Salts: Formed by neutralization, ionic compounds
  • Organic compounds: Alcohols, soaps, detergents, fuels
  • Oxides: Metal and non-metal oxides used in industry and construction

Examples

Example 1: Chemical Formula

Question: What is the chemical formula of baking soda?
Solution: \( NaHCO_3 \)

Example 2: Soap Reaction

Question: Reaction of soap with water hardness.
Solution: Soap reacts with \( Ca^{2+} \) or \( Mg^{2+} \) ions in hard water to form scum:
\( 2C_{17}H_{35}COONa + Ca^{2+} → (C_{17}H_{35}COO)_2Ca ↓ + 2Na^+ \)

Example 3: Neutralization

Question: Neutralize HCl with NaOH.
Solution: \( HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H_2O \)

Example 4: Detergent Advantage

Question: Why detergents are better than soap in hard water?
Solution: Detergents do not form scum with \( Ca^{2+} \) or \( Mg^{2+} \) ions.
