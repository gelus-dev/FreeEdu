Plant Biodiversity

Learn Plant Biodiversity and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Plant Biodiversity refers to the variety and variability of plant species, including algae, bryophytes, pteridophytes, gymnosperms, and angiosperms.

Key Concepts:

1. Algae:
• Simple, mostly aquatic plants
• Can be unicellular or multicellular
• Photosynthetic and produce oxygen
• Examples: Spirogyra, Chlorella

2. Bryophytes:
• Non-vascular plants
• Grow in moist environments
• Have rhizoids instead of roots
• Examples: Moss, Liverworts

3. Pteridophytes:
• Vascular plants without seeds
• Reproduce via spores
• Examples: Ferns, Horse-tails

4. Gymnosperms:
• Seed-bearing plants without flowers
• Seeds are exposed on cones
• Examples: Pine, Cedar

5. Angiosperms:
• Flowering plants with seeds enclosed in fruits
• Largest group of plants
• Examples: Rose, Mango, Wheat

6. Importance:
• Provides food, medicine, and raw materials
• Maintains ecological balance
• Supports habitat for animals and microbes
• Contributes to oxygen and carbon cycles

7. Threats:
• Deforestation and habitat loss
• Overexploitation
• Pollution and climate change
• Invasive species

8. Conservation:
• In situ: National parks, biosphere reserves
• Ex situ: Botanical gardens, seed banks
• Sustainable use and awareness programs
