Physical Constant Values

Learn Physical Constant Values ✅

Overview

Definition: Physical constants are fixed numerical values that appear in the fundamental laws of physics. They are universal in nature and do not change with time, location, or conditions.

Important Physical Constants:

1. Speed of Light (c): \( c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s} \)

2. Gravitational Constant (G): \( G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N·m²/kg²} \)

3. Planck’s Constant (h): \( h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J·s} \)

4. Elementary Charge (e): \( e = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C} \)

5. Mass of Electron (m_e): \( m_e = 9.109 \times 10^{-31} \, \text{kg} \)

6. Mass of Proton (m_p): \( m_p = 1.673 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg} \)

7. Permittivity of Free Space (ε₀): \( \varepsilon_0 = 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{F/m} \)

8. Permeability of Free Space (μ₀): \( \mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{H/m} \)

9. Boltzmann Constant (k_B): \( k_B = 1.381 \times 10^{-23} \, \text{J/K} \)

10. Avogadro Number (N_A): \( N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23} \, \text{mol}^{-1} \)

Secondary & High School Level Formulas Involving Constants:

• Gravitational Force: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \)
• Energy of Photon: \( E = h \nu \)
• Electric Force: \( F = \frac{1}{4 \pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)
• Kinetic Energy: \( KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \)
• Ideal Gas Law: \( PV = n k_B T \)
• Relation between μ₀, ε₀ and c: \( c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \varepsilon_0}} \)

Examples

Example 1: Gravitational Force

Given: Two masses \( m_1 = 2\,\text{kg}, m_2 = 3\,\text{kg} \), distance \( r = 2\,\text{m} \).
Find: Gravitational force between them.
Solution: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \frac{2*3}{2^2} = 1.001 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{N} \)

Example 2: Energy of Photon

Given: Light frequency \( \nu = 5 \times 10^{14} \, \text{Hz} \)
Find: Energy of photon.
Solution: \( E = h \nu = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} * 5 \times 10^{14} = 3.313 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \)

Example 3: Electric Force

Given: Charges \( q_1 = 1\,\mu C, q_2 = 2\,\mu C \), distance \( r = 0.5\,\text{m} \)
Find: Electrostatic force.
Solution: \( F = \frac{1}{4\pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} = 9 \times 10^9 * \frac{(1 \times 10^{-6})(2 \times 10^{-6})}{0.25} = 0.072 \, \text{N} \)
