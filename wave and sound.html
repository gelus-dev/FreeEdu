Wave and Sound

Learn Wave and Sound concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Waves are disturbances that transfer energy from one point to another without the transfer of matter. Sound is a longitudinal mechanical wave that propagates through a medium due to particle vibrations.

Types of Waves:

1. Mechanical Wave: Requires a medium, e.g., sound, water waves.

2. Electromagnetic Wave: Does not require a medium, e.g., light, radio waves.

3. Longitudinal Wave: Particles vibrate parallel to propagation, e.g., sound waves.

4. Transverse Wave: Particles vibrate perpendicular to propagation, e.g., light waves, string vibrations.

5. Progressive Wave: Energy moves forward, e.g., water wave.

6. Standing Wave: Result of superposition, nodes & antinodes form.

Wave Parameters:

• Wavelength \( \lambda \) — distance between successive crests/troughs
• Frequency \( f \) — number of oscillations per second
• Wave speed \( v \) — speed of energy propagation
• Period \( T = \frac{1}{f} \)
• Wave number \( k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \)
• Angular frequency \( \omega = 2\pi f \)

Basic Formulas:

• Wave speed: \( v = f \lambda \)
• Sound speed in air: \( v = 331 + 0.6T \) m/s (T in °C)
• Intensity: \( I = \frac{P}{A} \)
• Doppler Effect: \( f' = f \frac{v \pm v_o}{v \mp v_s} \)
• Fundamental frequency of string: \( f = \frac{1}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}} \)
• Resonance in air column (closed pipe): \( f_n = \frac{nv}{4L}, n=1,3,5... \)
• Resonance in air column (open pipe): \( f_n = \frac{nv}{2L}, n=1,2,3... \)

Examples

Example 1: Wave Speed

Given: Wavelength \( \lambda = 0.5 \) m, frequency \( f = 440 \) Hz
Find: Speed \( v \)
Solution: \( v = f \lambda = 440 \times 0.5 = 220 \text{ m/s} \)

Example 2: Doppler Effect

Given: Source moving towards observer at \( v_s = 20 \) m/s, \( f = 500 \) Hz, \( v = 340 \) m/s
Find: Observed frequency \( f' \)
Solution: \( f' = f \frac{v}{v - v_s} = 500 \frac{340}{340 - 20} = 500 \frac{340}{320} = 531.25 \text{ Hz} \)

Example 3: Fundamental Frequency of String

Given: Length \( L = 0.5 \) m, tension \( T = 100 \) N, mass per unit length \( \mu = 0.01 \) kg/m
Find: Fundamental frequency \( f \)
Solution: \( f = \frac{1}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}} = \frac{1}{1} \sqrt{\frac{100}{0.01}} = 100 \text{ Hz} \)

Example 4: Resonance in Closed Pipe

Given: Length \( L = 0.85 \) m, \( v = 340 \) m/s
Find: Fundamental frequency \( f_1 \)
Solution: \( f_1 = \frac{v}{4L} = \frac{340}{4 \times 0.85} = 100 \text{ Hz} \)
