Ecology

Learn Ecology and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Ecology is the branch of biology that studies the interactions between organisms and their environment, including the relationships among living organisms and the physical world.

Key Concepts:

1. Levels of Organization:
• Organism – an individual living being
• Population – group of individuals of the same species
• Community – different populations living together
• Ecosystem – community plus its physical environment
• Biosphere – global ecological system

2. Habitat and Niche:
• Habitat – place where an organism lives
• Niche – role or function of an organism in its ecosystem

3. Food Chain and Food Web:
• Producers – autotrophs, make their own food
• Consumers – herbivores, carnivores, omnivores
• Decomposers – break down organic matter
• Food web – interlinked food chains showing energy flow

4. Energy Flow and Trophic Levels:
• Energy flows from producers to consumers
• Trophic levels – producer, primary consumer, secondary consumer, tertiary consumer
• Energy decreases at each level (10% rule)

5. Biogeochemical Cycles:
• Water cycle – evaporation, condensation, precipitation
• Carbon cycle – photosynthesis, respiration, combustion
• Nitrogen cycle – fixation, nitrification, denitrification
• Oxygen cycle – photosynthesis and respiration

6. Population Ecology:
• Population size, density, and distribution
• Birth rate, death rate, immigration, emigration
• Carrying capacity – maximum sustainable population

7. Succession:
• Primary succession – colonization of bare land
• Secondary succession – recovery of disturbed ecosystems
• Climax community – stable, mature ecosystem

8. Biodiversity and Conservation:
• Genetic, species, and ecosystem diversity
• Conservation methods – protected areas, afforestation, sustainable use

9. Environmental Pollution:
• Air, water, soil pollution
• Effects on organisms and ecosystems
• Measures to reduce pollution
