Force and Motion

Learn Force and Motion concepts, operations and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Force is an interaction that changes the motion of a body. Motion refers to the change in position of a body with respect to time. Dynamics studies the relationship between force and motion.

Key Concepts:

1. Newton’s Laws of Motion:
• First Law (Law of Inertia)
• Second Law: \( \vec{F} = m \vec{a} \)
• Third Law: \( \vec{F}_{12} = - \vec{F}_{21} \)

2. Friction: Static & kinetic friction, \( f_s \le \mu_s N, f_k = \mu_k N \)

3. Circular Motion: Centripetal force \( F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} \), radial & tangential components

4. Work, Energy & Power: Work \( W = Fd\cos\theta \), Kinetic Energy \( KE = \frac12 mv^2 \), Potential Energy \( PE = mgh \), Power \( P = \frac{dW}{dt} \)

5. Momentum: Linear momentum \( \vec{p} = m \vec{v} \), Impulse \( \vec{J} = \Delta \vec{p} \)

6. Gravitation: Newton’s law of gravitation \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \), orbital motion, acceleration due to gravity \( g = \frac{GM}{R^2} \)

7. Projectile Motion: Horizontal & vertical motion independent, max height \( H = \frac{u_y^2}{2g} \), range \( R = \frac{u^2 \sin 2\theta}{g} \)

Examples

Example 1: Force on an Inclined Plane

Given: Mass \( m = 10\,kg \), incline \( \theta = 30^\circ \), \( g = 9.8\,m/s^2 \)
Find: Force down the plane
Solution: \( F = mg\sin\theta = 10*9.8*\frac12 = 49\,N \)

Example 2: Circular Motion

Given: Car of mass 1000 kg moving at 20 m/s on a circular track of radius 50 m
Find: Centripetal force
Solution: \( F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{1000*400}{50} = 8000\,N \)

Example 3: Work & Energy

Given: Force 50 N applied over 4 m at 60° to displacement
Find: Work done
Solution: \( W = Fd\cos\theta = 50*4*\cos60^\circ = 100\,J \)

Example 4: Collision (Elastic)

Given: Two masses 2 kg and 3 kg moving at 5 m/s and -2 m/s
Find: Velocity after elastic collision
Solution: Using elastic collision formulas: \( v_1' = 0.2\,m/s, v_2' = 3.8\,m/s \)
