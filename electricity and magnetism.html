Electricity and Magnetism

Learn Electricity and Magnetism concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Electricity is the study of electric charges, their motions, and interactions, while Magnetism deals with magnetic fields produced by moving charges and magnetic materials. Together, they form Electromagnetism, a fundamental branch of physics describing forces, fields, and energy transfer.

Key Concepts:

1. Electric Charge (q): Fundamental property of matter, unit: Coulomb (C)

2. Electric Current (I): Flow of charges, \( I = \frac{dq}{dt} \)

3. Voltage (V): Electric potential difference, \( V = \frac{W}{q} \)

4. Resistance (R): Opposition to current, \( R = \rho \frac{L}{A} \)

5. Ohm’s Law: \( V = IR \)

6. Series and Parallel Circuits:
• Series: \( R_{eq} = R_1 + R_2 + ... \), \( V = V_1 + V_2 + ... \), \( I = I_1 = I_2 ... \)
• Parallel: \( \frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + ... \), \( I = I_1 + I_2 + ... \), \( V = V_1 = V_2 ... \)

Energy and Power:

• Electrical Power: \( P = VI = I^2 R = \frac{V^2}{R} \)
• Energy: \( W = VIt = I^2 R t \)

Examples

Example 1: Series Resistance

Given: \( R_1 = 5 \Omega, R_2 = 10 \Omega \) in series.
Find: Equivalent resistance.
Solution: \( R_{eq} = R_1 + R_2 = 15 \Omega \)

Example 2: Parallel Resistance

Given: \( R_1 = 6 \Omega, R_2 = 3 \Omega \) in parallel.
Find: Equivalent resistance.
Solution: \( \frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow R_{eq} = 2 \Omega \)

Example 3: Magnetic Force

Given: Charge \( q = 2C \), velocity \( v = 3 m/s \) perpendicular to \( B = 4 T \)
Find: Magnetic force.
Solution: \( F = qvB = 2*3*4 = 24 N \)

Example 4: Electric Power

Given: \( I = 2A, R = 5\Omega \)
Find: Power dissipated.
Solution: \( P = I^2 R = 2^2 * 5 = 20 W \)
