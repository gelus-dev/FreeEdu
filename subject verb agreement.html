Subject Verb Agreement

Learn Subject Verb Agreement and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Subject-Verb Agreement means that the subject and the verb in a sentence must agree in number (singular or plural). Singular subjects take singular verbs, and plural subjects take plural verbs.

Key Concepts :

1. Singular and Plural Subjects:
• Singular subject → Singular verb (He runs)
• Plural subject → Plural verb (They run)

2. With 'and':
• Subjects joined by 'and' usually take a plural verb (Ram and Sita are friends).
• If both refer to the same person/thing, singular verb is used (Bread and butter is my favorite).

3. With 'or', 'either...or', 'neither...nor':
• Verb agrees with the subject closest to it.
• Example: Either Ram or his friends are coming.
• Example: Neither the teacher nor the student was present.

4. Indefinite Pronouns:
• Each, Everyone, Somebody, Anybody → Singular verb (Everyone is happy).
• Few, Many, Several → Plural verb (Many are invited).

5. Collective Nouns:
• If group acts as one → Singular verb (The team is winning).
• If individuals are emphasized → Plural verb (The team are arguing among themselves).

6. Distance, Time, Money:
• Singular verb used when considered as a unit.
• Example: Ten kilometers is a long walk.
• Example: Five dollars is not enough.

7. Special Cases:
• The number → Singular verb (The number of students is high).
• A number → Plural verb (A number of students are absent).
