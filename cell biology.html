Cell Biology

Learn Cell Biology and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Cell biology is the branch of biology that studies the structure, function, and behavior of cells, which are the basic units of life.

Key Concepts (School Level Important):

1. Cell Theory: All living organisms are made of cells, the cell is the basic unit of life, and all cells arise from pre-existing cells.

2. Types of Cells:
• Prokaryotic cells – lack a true nucleus (bacteria)
• Eukaryotic cells – have a true nucleus (plants, animals, fungi)

3. Cell Organelles (Secondary & High School Important):
• Nucleus – control center of the cell
• Mitochondria – powerhouse of the cell (site of respiration)
• Ribosomes – site of protein synthesis
• Endoplasmic Reticulum (Rough/Smooth) – transport and synthesis
• Golgi Apparatus – packaging and secretion
• Lysosomes – digestion of waste (suicidal bags)
• Chloroplast – photosynthesis (plants only)
• Vacuole – storage of water and food (large in plants)
• Cell Wall – provides support (plants only)
• Cell Membrane – selectively permeable, controls movement

4. Differences:
• Plant vs Animal cells – plant cells have cell wall, chloroplast, and large vacuoles
• Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic – prokaryotes lack a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles

5. Cell Division:
• Mitosis – produces identical cells, important for growth and repair (secondary school important)
• Meiosis – produces gametes, results in genetic variation (high school important)
