Water

Learn Water and examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Water is a transparent, tasteless, odorless, and nearly colorless chemical substance, H₂O, which is the main constituent of Earth's hydrosphere and essential for all known forms of life.

Properties of Water:

1. Physical Properties: Colorless, tasteless, odorless; boiling point 100°C; melting point 0°C; density 1 g/cm³ at 4°C.

2. Chemical Properties: Acts as an acid and base (amphoteric), reacts with metals, non-metals, oxides, and salts.

3. Solvent Properties: Universal solvent, dissolves ionic and polar covalent compounds.

Secondary/High School Level Concepts:

• Hard and Soft Water: Hard water contains Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺; soft water does not.
• Boiling and Freezing Point Elevation/Depression: \( \Delta T_b = K_b m \), \( \Delta T_f = K_f m \)
• Electrolysis of Water: \( 2H_2O(l) \xrightarrow{\text{electric current}} 2H_2(g) + O_2(g) \)
• Oxidation and Reduction Reactions: Water can act as oxidizing/reducing agent.

High School Level Formulas:

• Molarity: \( M = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{litres of solution}} \)
• Mole Fraction: \( X_A = \frac{n_A}{\sum n_i} \)
• Molality: \( m = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{kg of solvent}} \)
• Colligative properties: \( \Delta T_b = K_b m i \), \( \Delta T_f = K_f m i \), \( \Pi = MRT \) (osmotic pressure)
• Density: \( \rho = \frac{mass}{volume} \)

Examples

Example 1: Boiling Point Elevation

Given: 1 mol of NaCl dissolved in 1 kg of water. \( K_b = 0.512°C·kg/mol \)
Find: Boiling point elevation.
Solution: \( \Delta T_b = i K_b m = 2 × 0.512 × 1 = 1.024°C \). Boiling point = 101.024°C

Example 2: Electrolysis of Water

Given: Electric current passed through water.
Find: Volume ratio of H₂ and O₂ produced.
Solution: \( 2H_2O → 2H_2 + O_2 \). Volume ratio H₂:O₂ = 2:1

Example 3: Mole Fraction

Given: 18 g of H₂O and 58 g of NaCl.
Find: Mole fraction of H₂O.
Solution: Moles H₂O = 18/18 =1, Moles NaCl = 58/58.5 ≈ 0.99, Mole fraction H₂O = 1/(1+0.99) ≈ 0.502

Example 4: Hardness of Water

Given: 120 mg of CaCO₃ in 1 L of water.
Find: Hardness in ppm.
Solution: Hardness (ppm) = (120 mg/L) = 120 ppm
