Heat

Learn Heat concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Heat is a form of energy transferred between bodies due to a temperature difference. It is measured in joules (J) in SI units.

Key Concepts:

1. Specific Heat Capacity (c): Heat required to raise 1 kg of substance by 1 K. \( Q = mc\Delta T \)

2. Latent Heat: Heat required for phase change without temperature change. \( Q = mL \)

3. Thermal Expansion: \( \Delta L = \alpha L \Delta T \), \( \Delta V = \beta V \Delta T \)

4. Heat Transfer: Conduction: \( Q = \frac{k A \Delta T t}{d} \)
Convection and Radiation (Stefan-Boltzmann law): \( P = \sigma A T^4 \)

High School Level Formulas:

• \( Q = mc\Delta T \)
• \( Q = mL \)
• \( \Delta L = \alpha L \Delta T \)
• \( \Delta V = \beta V \Delta T \)
• \( P = \sigma A T^4 \)
• \( Q = \frac{k A \Delta T t}{d} \)

Examples

Example 1: Heat Required to Raise Temperature

Given: 2 kg of water, \( c = 4200\ J/kg.K \), \( \Delta T = 30 K \)
Find: Heat required
Solution: \( Q = mc\Delta T = 2*4200*30 = 252,000\ J \)

Example 2: Heat for Phase Change

Given: 0.5 kg of ice, \( L_f = 334,000\ J/kg \)
Find: Heat required to melt ice
Solution: \( Q = mL = 0.5*334,000 = 167,000\ J \)

Example 3: Thermal Expansion

Given: Rod of length 2 m, \( \alpha = 12 \times 10^{-6}/K \), \( \Delta T = 50 K \)
Find: Increase in length
Solution: \( \Delta L = \alpha L \Delta T = 12 \times 10^{-6}*2*50 = 0.0012\ m \)

Example 4: Heat Conduction

Given: Copper rod, \( k = 400 W/m.K \), area \( A = 0.01 m^2 \), \( \Delta T = 50 K \), thickness \( d = 0.05 m \), time \( t = 100 s \)
Find: Heat conducted
Solution: \( Q = \frac{k A \Delta T t}{d} = \frac{400*0.01*50*100}{0.05} = 4,000,000\ J \)
