Work, Energy and Power

Learn Work, Energy and Power concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Work is done when a force is applied on an object and the object is displaced in the direction of the force. Energy is the capacity to do work. Power is the rate at which work is done.

Formulas:

• Work: \( W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d} = Fd\cos\theta \)
• Kinetic Energy: \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \)
• Potential Energy: \( PE = mgh \)
• Mechanical Energy: \( E = KE + PE \)
• Work-Energy Theorem: \( W_{net} = \Delta KE \)
• Power: \( P = \frac{W}{t} = Fv\cos\theta \)
• Efficiency: \( \eta = \frac{Useful\ Work}{Total\ Work} \times 100\% \)
• Gravitational Potential Energy (general): \( U = -\frac{GMm}{r} \)

Concepts:

• Positive Work: Force and displacement in same direction.
• Negative Work: Force and displacement in opposite direction.
• Conservative Forces: Work done depends only on initial and final positions (e.g., gravity, spring).
• Non-Conservative Forces: Work depends on path (e.g., friction).

Examples

Example 1: Work Done by Constant Force

Given: A force \( F = 10\,\mathrm{N} \) acts on an object moving \( d = 5\,\mathrm{m} \) in the direction of the force.
Find: Work done.
Solution: \( W = Fd\cos\theta = 10*5*1 = 50\,\mathrm{J} \)

Example 2: Kinetic Energy

Given: Mass \( m = 2\,\mathrm{kg} \), velocity \( v = 3\,\mathrm{m/s} \)
Find: Kinetic energy.
Solution: \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{1}{2}*2*3^2 = 9\,\mathrm{J} \)

Example 3: Potential Energy

Given: Mass \( m = 5\,\mathrm{kg} \), height \( h = 10\,\mathrm{m} \), \( g = 9.8\,\mathrm{m/s^2} \)
Find: Gravitational potential energy.
Solution: \( PE = mgh = 5*9.8*10 = 490\,\mathrm{J} \)

Example 4: Power

Given: Work done \( W = 100\,\mathrm{J} \), time \( t = 20\,\mathrm{s} \)
Find: Power.
Solution: \( P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{100}{20} = 5\,\mathrm{W} \)

Example 5: Work-Energy Theorem

Given: A force accelerates a 2 kg mass from rest to 4 m/s.
Find: Work done.
Solution: \( W = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}*2*4^2 - 0 = 16\,\mathrm{J} \)
