Atoms and Molecules

Learn Atoms and Molecules concepts and examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Atoms are the smallest units of matter that retain the properties of an element. Molecules are formed when two or more atoms chemically bond together.

Key Concepts:

1. Atomic Mass (m): Mass of an atom in atomic mass units (u).
2. Molecular Mass (M): Sum of atomic masses of all atoms in a molecule.
3. Avogadro’s Number (Nₐ): \( 6.022 \times 10^{23} \) particles/mol.
4. Molar Mass (Mₘ): Mass of 1 mole of substance in grams, \( M_m = \text{molecular mass in u (g/mol)} \).

Important Formulas:

• Number of moles: \( n = \frac{m}{M_m} \)
• Number of molecules: \( N = n N_A \)
• Mass from moles: \( m = n M_m \)
• Empirical formula calculation: \(\text{Ratio of atoms} = \frac{\text{mass %}}{\text{atomic mass}}\)
• Molecular formula: \( \text{Molecular formula} = (\text{Empirical formula}) \times n \), where \( n = \frac{\text{Molecular mass}}{\text{Empirical formula mass}} \)

Secondary & High School Level Extensions:

• Gram-atom concept: 1 mole of atoms = atomic mass in grams.
• Gram-molecule: 1 mole of molecules = molecular mass in grams.
• Density relation: \( \rho = \frac{MP}{RT} \) (ideal gas law for gases)
• Partial pressures: \( P_i = \frac{n_i RT}{V} \)
• Stoichiometry calculations in chemical reactions using mole concept.

Examples

Example 1: Number of Moles

Given: 18 g of water (\( H_2O \)), \( M_m = 18 \text{ g/mol} \)
Find: Number of moles.
Solution: \( n = \frac{m}{M_m} = \frac{18}{18} = 1 \text{ mole} \)

Example 2: Number of Molecules

Given: 2 moles of \( CO_2 \).
Find: Number of molecules.
Solution: \( N = n N_A = 2 \times 6.022 \times 10^{23} = 1.2044 \times 10^{24} \) molecules

Example 3: Empirical Formula

Given: Compound has 40% C, 6.7% H, 53.3% O.
Solution:
C: \( 40/12 = 3.33 \)
H: \( 6.7/1 = 6.7 \)
O: \( 53.3/16 = 3.33 \)
Divide by smallest: C:H:O = 1:2:1
Empirical formula: \( CH_2O \)

Example 4: Molecular Formula

Given: Empirical formula \( CH_2O \), molecular mass = 180 g/mol.
Find: Molecular formula.
Solution: Empirical mass = 12+2+16=30 g/mol, \( n = 180/30 = 6 \)
Molecular formula = \( (CH_2O) \times 6 = C_6H_{12}O_6 \)
