Question Tag

Learn Question Tag and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Question Tags are short questions added at the end of a statement to confirm information or seek agreement. They usually mirror the auxiliary/modal verb and subject of the main sentence.

Key Concepts :

1. Structure:
• Positive statement → Negative question tag
• Negative statement → Positive question tag
• Example: You are coming, aren’t you? / She isn’t late, is she?

2. Auxiliary/Modal Verbs:
• Use the same auxiliary/modal verb as in the main sentence.
• Example: He can swim, can’t he? / She will go, won’t she?

3. No Auxiliary in Main Verb:
• If the main verb is simple present or simple past without auxiliary, use do/does/did.
• Example: You like coffee, don’t you? / He went home, didn’t he?

4. Special Subjects:
• I am → aren’t I?
• Let’s → shall we?
• Imperatives → will you?/won’t you?
• There is/are → isn’t there?/aren’t there?
• Some/any → aren’t there?/isn’t there? depending on context
• Nobody/none → did they?/aren’t they? depending on tense

5. Agreement Rules:
• The tag always refers to the subject of the main clause.
• Pronouns in the tag match the subject of the main sentence (he, she, it, they, I, we, you).
• The tense of the tag mirrors the tense of the main clause.

6. Special Exceptions:
• “I am” always takes aren’t I? (not amn’t I)
• Imperatives usually take will you? or won’t you? rather than do/does
• Negative imperatives: “Don’t touch this, ___?” → will you?
• With “let’s” → shall we?
• With indefinite pronouns (everyone, nobody, someone): treat as third person singular
Example: Everyone is ready, aren’t they?

C) don’t she
