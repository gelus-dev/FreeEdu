Human System

Learn Human System and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: The human body system is a group of organs working together to perform specific vital functions necessary for survival and maintaining homeostasis.

Main Human Body Systems (School Level Important):

1. Digestive System: Breaks down food into nutrients (mouth, stomach, intestine, liver, pancreas).

2. Respiratory System: Exchanges oxygen and carbon dioxide (nose, trachea, lungs).

3. Circulatory System: Transports blood, oxygen, nutrients (heart, blood vessels, blood).

4. Excretory System: Removes waste from body (kidneys, ureters, bladder, skin).

5. Nervous System: Controls and coordinates activities (brain, spinal cord, nerves).

6. Musculoskeletal System: Provides support and movement (bones, muscles, joints).

7. Endocrine System: Produces hormones to regulate body functions (pituitary, thyroid, pancreas).

8. Reproductive System: Enables reproduction (male: testes, female: ovaries, uterus).

9. Immune/Lymphatic System: Defends against diseases (lymph nodes, spleen, white blood cells).

10. Integumentary System: Protects body (skin, hair, nails, glands).

Examples

Example 1: Digestion of Food

Process: Food is chewed in the mouth → mixed with saliva → goes to stomach where proteins are broken down by gastric juice → moves to small intestine where bile and pancreatic juice help digestion → nutrients absorbed in small intestine → waste sent to large intestine.

Example 2: Blood Circulation

Process: The heart pumps oxygenated blood from left ventricle → through arteries → to body cells → deoxygenated blood returns via veins → right atrium → right ventricle → pumped to lungs for oxygen exchange → oxygenated blood returns to left atrium → cycle continues.

Example 3: Breathing Mechanism

Process: During inhalation, diaphragm contracts → chest cavity enlarges → air enters lungs. During exhalation, diaphragm relaxes → chest cavity reduces → air rich in carbon dioxide is expelled.

Example 4: Excretion

Process: Blood is filtered in kidneys → urea and salts removed → urine passes through ureters → stored in bladder → excreted through urethra.

Example 5: Reflex Action

Process: When we touch a hot object → sensory nerves send signal to spinal cord → spinal cord sends immediate response via motor nerves → muscles contract and hand is withdrawn quickly (without waiting for brain’s decision).
