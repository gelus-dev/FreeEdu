Optics

Learn Optics concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Optics is the branch of physics that studies the behavior and properties of light, including its interactions with matter and the construction of instruments that use or detect it.

Branches of Optics:

1. Geometrical Optics: Studies light propagation in terms of rays. Deals with reflection, refraction, lenses, mirrors.

2. Physical Optics: Considers the wave nature of light. Deals with interference, diffraction, polarization.

3. Quantum Optics: Studies light at photon level, quantum properties, and interaction with atoms.

Important Concepts & Formulas:

• Snell's Law: \( n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2 \)
• Mirror Formula: \( \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v} \)
• Lens Formula: \( \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} \)
• Magnification: \( m = \frac{v}{u} = \frac{h_i}{h_o} \)
• Critical Angle: \( \theta_c = \sin^{-1} \frac{n_2}{n_1} \) (for \( n_1 > n_2 \))
• Power of Lens: \( P = \frac{100}{f\text{(cm)}} \)
• Interference Condition: \( n \lambda = d \sin \theta \) (for maxima)
• Diffraction: \( a \sin \theta = m \lambda \) (single slit)
• Polarization: \( I = I_0 \cos^2 \theta \) (Malus' law)

Examples

Example 1: Lens Formula

Given: Object distance \( u = 30\,\text{cm} \), focal length \( f = 10\,\text{cm} \)
Find: Image distance \( v \)
Solution: \( \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{30} = \frac{2}{30} \Rightarrow v = 15\,\text{cm} \)

Example 2: Mirror Magnification

Given: Image height \( h_i = 6\,\text{cm} \), object height \( h_o = 4\,\text{cm} \)
Find: Magnification \( m \)
Solution: \( m = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = \frac{6}{4} = 1.5 \)

Example 3: Critical Angle

Given: Refractive index of water \( n_1 = 1.33 \), air \( n_2 = 1 \)
Find: Critical angle \( \theta_c \)
Solution: \( \theta_c = \sin^{-1} \frac{n_2}{n_1} = \sin^{-1} \frac{1}{1.33} \approx 48.75^\circ \)

Example 4: Interference Maxima

Given: Wavelength \( \lambda = 600\,\text{nm} \), slit separation \( d = 0.2\,\text{mm} \), angle \( \theta \) for first maxima
Solution: \( d \sin \theta = n \lambda \Rightarrow 0.2\times10^{-3} \sin \theta = 1 \times 600\times10^{-9} \Rightarrow \theta \approx 0.172^\circ \)
