Tense Sequence

Learn Tense Sequence and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Tense Sequence refers to the rule of maintaining logical relationship of tenses between the main clause and subordinate clause in a sentence.

Key Concepts :

1. General Rule:
• If the verb in the main clause is in past tense, the verb in the subordinate clause usually also goes to past tense.
• If the verb in the main clause is in present or future tense, the verb in the subordinate clause can be in any tense as required.

2. Present/Future in Main Clause:
• Subordinate clause can take any tense.
• Example: He says that he is happy. / He will say that he was ill.

3. Past in Main Clause:
• Subordinate clause usually takes past tense.
• Example: He said that he was ill.

4. Exceptions to Past Rule:
• Universal truths remain in present tense even if the main clause is past.
• Example: He said that the earth is round.
• If the subordinate clause shows a future event relative to the past, "would/should + V1" is used.
• Example: He said that he would come.

5. Reported Speech Application:
• Direct speech → Indirect speech follows tense sequence.
• Example: Direct: He said, "I am tired." → Indirect: He said that he was tired.
