Origin and Evolution

Learn Origin and Evolution and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Origin and Evolution in biology is the study of how life began on Earth and how species have changed over time through processes like natural selection, mutation, and adaptation.

Key Concepts:

1. Origin of Life: Life originated from simple molecules through chemical evolution, forming complex organic compounds that eventually led to primitive cells.

2. Theories of Origin: • Primordial Soup Theory: Life began in a “soup” of organic molecules.
• RNA World Hypothesis: RNA molecules were first genetic material.
• Panspermia: Life came from extraterrestrial sources.

3. Evolution: Gradual change in species over generations leading to diversity of life forms.

4. Mechanisms of Evolution: • Natural Selection: Survival of the fittest.
• Mutation: Random changes in DNA.
• Genetic Drift: Random changes in allele frequency.
• Gene Flow: Movement of genes between populations.

5. Evidence of Evolution: Fossil records, comparative anatomy, embryology, molecular biology, and biogeography.

6. Speciation: Formation of new species through reproductive isolation (allopatric, sympatric, and peripatric speciation).

7. Human Evolution: Evolution from early primates to Homo sapiens through transitional species like Australopithecus, Homo habilis, and Homo erectus.

Examples

Example 1: Natural Selection

Given: A population of beetles with green and brown colors, brown beetles survive predation better.
Result: Over generations, brown beetles become more common.

Example 2: Speciation

Given: A bird population splits into two geographically isolated groups.
Result: Genetic differences accumulate, resulting in two distinct species.

Example 3: Fossil Evidence

Observation: Transitional fossils like Archaeopteryx show characteristics of both dinosaurs and birds.
Significance: Supports evolutionary links between groups.

Example 4: Mutation

Given: A mutation in a flower gene changes petal color.
Effect: May provide reproductive advantage if pollinators prefer the new color.
