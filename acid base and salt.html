Acid,Base and Salt

Learn Acid,Base and Salt and examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Acids are substances that donate protons (H⁺ ions) in solution, Bases are substances that accept protons or donate OH⁻ ions, and Salts are ionic compounds formed from the neutralization of an acid and a base.

Types of Acids:

1. Strong Acids: Completely ionize in water, e.g., HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃

2. Weak Acids: Partially ionize in water, e.g., CH₃COOH, HF

Types of Bases:

1. Strong Bases: Completely dissociate in water, e.g., NaOH, KOH

2. Weak Bases: Partially dissociate in water, e.g., NH₃

Types of Salts:

1. Neutral Salt: Formed from strong acid + strong base, e.g., NaCl

2. Acidic Salt: Formed from strong acid + weak base, e.g., NH₄Cl

3. Basic Salt: Formed from weak acid + strong base, e.g., Na₂CO₃

Important Concepts:

• pH: \( \text{pH} = -\log[H^+] \)
• pOH: \( \text{pOH} = -\log[OH^-] \)
• Relation: \( \text{pH + pOH = 14} \)
• Neutralization: \( \text{Acid + Base → Salt + H₂O} \)

Examples

Example 1: pH of Strong Acid

Given: 0.01 M HCl
Solution: \( \text{pH} = -\log[H^+] = -\log(0.01) = 2 \)

Example 2: pH of Weak Acid

Given: 0.1 M CH₃COOH, \( K_a = 1.8 \times 10^{-5} \)
Solution: \( [H^+] = \sqrt{K_a \cdot C} = \sqrt{1.8 \times 10^{-5} \cdot 0.1} = 1.34 \times 10^{-3} \)
\( \text{pH} = -\log(1.34 \times 10^{-3}) \approx 2.87 \)

Example 3: Neutralization Reaction

Given: 25 mL 0.1 M HCl reacts with 25 mL 0.1 M NaOH
Solution: \( \text{HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O} \), solution is neutral, \( \text{pH} = 7 \)
