Statistics

Learn about Statistics concepts, operations and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Statistics is the branch of mathematics that deals with collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data to make decisions.

Types of Statistics:

1. Descriptive Statistics: Summarizes and describes data.
Example: Mean, Median, Mode, Standard Deviation

2. Inferential Statistics: Makes predictions or inferences about a population based on sample data.
Example: Hypothesis testing, Confidence intervals

Measures of Central Tendency:

• Mean (Arithmetic Average): \(\bar{x} = \frac{\Sigma x_i}{n}\)
• Median: Middle value when data is arranged in order
• Mode: Value occurring most frequently
• Weighted Mean: \(\bar{x}_w = \frac{\Sigma w_i x_i}{\Sigma w_i}\)

Measures of Dispersion:

• Range: R = Max - Min
• Variance: Population: \(\sigma^2 = \frac{\Sigma (x_i - \mu)^2}{N}\)
Sample: \(s^2 = \frac{\Sigma (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n-1}\)
• Standard Deviation: Population: \(\sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2}\), Sample: \(s = \sqrt{s^2}\)
• Coefficient of Variation: \(CV = \frac{SD}{Mean} \times 100\%\)

Moments, Skewness & Kurtosis:

• Raw Moment (r-th order): \(m_r = \frac{\Sigma x_i^r}{n}\)
• Central Moment (r-th order): \(\mu_r = \frac{\Sigma (x_i - \bar{x})^r}{n}\)
• Skewness: \(Sk = \frac{\mu_3}{\sigma^3}\)
• Kurtosis: \(Ku = \frac{\mu_4}{\sigma^4}\)

Correlation & Regression:

• Correlation Coefficient (r): \(r = \frac{\Sigma (x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\sqrt{\Sigma (x_i - \bar{x})^2 \Sigma (y_i - \bar{y})^2}}\)
• Regression Line of y on x: \(y = a + bx, b = \frac{\Sigma (x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\Sigma (x_i - \bar{x})^2}, a = \bar{y} - b\bar{x}\)
• Regression Line of x on y: \(x = a' + b'y, b' = \frac{\Sigma (x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\Sigma (y_i - \bar{y})^2}, a' = \bar{x} - b'\bar{y}\)

Examples

Example 1: Mean

Given: Data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10
Solution: Mean = (2+4+6+8+10)/5 = 30/5 = 6

Example 2: Median

Given: Data: 7, 3, 9, 5, 1
Solution: Arrange: 1,3,5,7,9 → Median = 5

Example 3: Mode

Given: Data: 4, 4, 6, 7, 4, 9
Solution: Mode = 4 (occurs most frequently)

Example 4: Standard Deviation

Given: Data: 2, 4, 6
Solution: Mean = 4, Variance = [(2-4)² + (4-4)² + (6-4)²]/3 = (4+0+4)/3 = 8/3 → SD = √(8/3) ≈ 1.632

Example 5: Correlation Coefficient

Given: X: 1,2,3; Y: 2,3,5
Solution: r = [Σ(x_i-2)(y_i-10/3)] / √[Σ(x_i-2)² Σ(y_i-10/3)²] → r ≈ 0.981

Example 6: Regression Line of y on x

Given: X:1,2,3; Y:2,3,5
Solution: b = Σ(x_i-2)(y_i-10/3)/Σ(x_i-2)² = 1.5, a = 10/3 - 1.5*2 = -1/6 → y = -1/6 + 1.5x
