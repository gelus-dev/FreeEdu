Pressure

Learn Pressure concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Pressure is defined as the normal force acting per unit area of a surface.

Formulas:

• Basic Pressure: \( P = \frac{F}{A} \)

• Hydrostatic Pressure: \( P = h \rho g \)

• Absolute Pressure: \( P_{abs} = P_{atm} + P_{gauge} \)

• Pascal’s Law: \( P_1 = P_2 \) (in a confined fluid)

• Hydraulic Lift: \( \frac{F_1}{A_1} = \frac{F_2}{A_2} \)

• Buoyant Force (Archimedes): \( F_b = \rho_{fluid} g V_{displaced} \)

• Surface Tension Pressure Difference:
– For liquid drop: \( \Delta P = \frac{2T}{r} \)
– For soap bubble: \( \Delta P = \frac{4T}{r} \)

• Capillary Rise: \( h = \frac{2T \cos\theta}{r \rho g} \)

• Boyle’s Law: \( P V = \text{constant} \) (isothermal)

• Pressure in gases (kinetic theory): \( P = \frac{1}{3}\rho v_{rms}^2 \)

Examples

Example 1: Basic Pressure

Given: A force of 200 N acts on an area of 0.5 m².
Solution: \( P = \frac{200}{0.5} = 400 \, \text{Pa} \)

Example 2: Hydrostatic Pressure

Given: Depth = 10 m, density of water = 1000 kg/m³, g = 9.8 m/s².
Solution: \( P = \rho g h = 1000 \times 9.8 \times 10 = 9.8 \times 10^4 \, \text{Pa} \)

Example 3: Hydraulic Lift

Given: A1 = 0.01 m², A2 = 0.5 m², F1 = 100 N.
Solution: \( \frac{F_1}{A_1} = \frac{F_2}{A_2} \)
\( \frac{100}{0.01} = \frac{F_2}{0.5} \)
\( F_2 = 5000 \, \text{N} \)

Example 4: Buoyancy

Given: Volume displaced = 0.02 m³, fluid density = 1000 kg/m³.
Solution: \( F_b = \rho g V = 1000 \times 9.8 \times 0.02 = 196 \, N \)

Example 5: Capillarity

Given: T = 0.072 N/m, θ = 0°, r = 0.5 mm, ρ = 1000 kg/m³.
Solution: \( h = \frac{2T \cos\theta}{r\rho g} = \frac{2 \times 0.072}{0.0005 \times 1000 \times 9.8} \approx 0.029 \, m = 2.9 \, cm \)
