Probability

Learn about Probability concepts, operations and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Probability is the measure of the likelihood of an event occurring. It ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).

Key Concepts:

1. Experiment: Any process that produces outcomes.
Example: Rolling a die.

2. Sample Space (S): The set of all possible outcomes.
Example: S = {1,2,3,4,5,6} for a die.

3. Event: Any subset of sample space.
Example: Getting an even number → E = {2,4,6}.

4. Mutually Exclusive & Independent Events: Understanding relationships between events for addition/multiplication rules.

Formulas:

• \( P(E) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total outcomes}} \)

• Complement: \( P(E') = 1 - P(E) \)

• Addition Rule: \( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \)

• Multiplication Rule (Independent): \( P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B) \)

• Conditional Probability: \( P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \)

• Bayes’ Theorem: \( P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \)

• Probability of at least one: \( P(\text{at least one}) = 1 - P(\text{none}) \)

Examples

Example 1: Complex Event Probability

Given: Draw 2 cards from a deck.
Find: Probability of drawing one red and one black.
Solution: \( P = \frac{26}{52} \cdot \frac{26}{51} + \frac{26}{52} \cdot \frac{26}{51} = \frac{26}{51} \)

Example 2: Conditional Probability

Given: Bag has 5 red and 3 blue balls. Pick 2 balls.
Find: Probability second ball is red given first is blue.
Solution: \( P(R_2|B_1) = \frac{5}{7} \)

Example 3: At least one event

Given: Toss 3 coins.
Find: Probability of getting at least one tail.
Solution: \( P(\text{at least one T}) = 1 - P(\text{no T}) = 1 - (\frac{1}{2})^3 = \frac{7}{8} \)

Example 4: Bayes’ Theorem

Given: 1% defective bulbs, test detects 95% defective, 2% false positive.
Find: Probability bulb is defective if test is positive.
Solution: \( P(D|T^+) = \frac{0.95*0.01}{0.95*0.01 + 0.02*0.99} \approx 0.32 \)

Practice Questions

Question 1:

Two dice are rolled. What is the probability that the sum is 7 or 11?

Question 2:

A bag has 4 white, 5 black, 6 red balls. One ball is drawn at random. Find the probability it is white or red.

Question 3:

Three coins are tossed. Find the probability of getting exactly 2 heads.

Question 4:

From a deck of 52 cards, 2 cards are drawn without replacement. Find probability both are kings.

Question 5:

Box has 3 defective and 7 good items. Two items selected randomly. Probability exactly one defective?

Question 6:

Probability of passing exam is 0.7. Find probability of passing exactly 2 out of 3 attempts.

Question 7:

A test for a disease detects correctly 98% diseased and has 5% false positive. Disease prevalence 1%. Find probability patient has disease if test positive.

Question 8:

Four cards drawn from a deck. Find probability of getting 2 hearts and 2 clubs.

Question 9:

Two events A and B are independent with P(A)=0.6, P(B)=0.5. Find P(A∪B).

Question 10:

Probability it rains today is 0.4 and tomorrow is 0.5. Find probability it rains exactly one day.
