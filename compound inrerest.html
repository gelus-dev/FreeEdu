Compound Interest

Learn about Compound Interest concepts, operations and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Compound Interest (CI) is the interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods. It is widely used in finance and competitive exams.

Formula:

• CI Formula: \( A = P \left(1 + \frac{r}{100}\right)^n \)
Where:
\( A \) = Amount after n periods
\( P \) = Principal
\( r \) = Rate of interest per period (%)
\( n \) = Number of periods

• Compound Interest: \( CI = A - P = P \left( \left(1 + \frac{r}{100}\right)^n - 1 \right) \)

Types:

• Annually Compounded: Interest calculated once per year.
• Half-Yearly Compounded: Interest calculated twice per year, \( r \) and \( n \) adjusted accordingly.
• Quarterly Compounded: Interest calculated four times per year.
• Monthly Compounded: Interest calculated twelve times per year.

Approximation for Small Interest:

When interest is small, \( CI \approx P \cdot n \cdot \frac{r}{100} + \frac{P \cdot n(n-1) r^2}{20000} \)

Examples

Example 1: Simple Compound Interest

Given: \( P = 1000, r = 10\%, n = 2 \)
Find: Compound Interest
Solution: \( A = 1000 (1 + 0.10)^2 = 1000 \cdot 1.21 = 1210 \)
\( CI = 1210 - 1000 = 210 \)

Example 2: Half-Yearly Compounding

Given: \( P = 5000, r = 12\%, n = 2 \text{ years, half-yearly} \)
Find: Amount
Solution: Half-yearly rate \( = 6\% \), periods \( = 4 \)
\( A = 5000 (1 + 0.06)^4 = 5000 \cdot 1.2625 = 6312.5 \)
\( CI = 6312.5 - 5000 = 1312.5 \)

Example 3: CI Approximation

Given: \( P = 2000, r = 5\%, n = 3 \)
Solution: \( CI \approx 2000 \cdot 3 \cdot 0.05 + \frac{2000 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 25}{20000} = 300 + 15 = 315 \)

Example 4: Quarterly Compounding

Given: \( P = 10000, r = 8\%, n = 1 \text{ year, quarterly} \)
Solution: Quarterly rate \( = 2\% \), periods \( = 4 \)
\( A = 10000 (1 + 0.02)^4 = 10000 \cdot 1.08243216 \approx 10824.32 \)
\( CI = 10824.32 - 10000 = 824.32 \)
