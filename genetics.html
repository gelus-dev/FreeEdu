Genetics

Learn Genetics and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Genetics is the branch of biology that studies heredity and variation in living organisms, focusing on how traits are passed from parents to offspring.

Key Concepts (School Level Important):

1. Mendel’s Laws :
• Law of Dominance – one allele may mask the effect of another.
• Law of Segregation – alleles separate during gamete formation.
• Law of Independent Assortment – traits are inherited independently of each other.

2. Gene and Allele:
• Gene – unit of heredity controlling traits.
• Alleles – alternative forms of a gene (dominant/recessive).

3. Chromosomes (Secondary & High School Important):
• Humans have 46 chromosomes (23 pairs).
• Autosomes – 22 pairs control body traits.
• Sex chromosomes – 1 pair (XX in females, XY in males).

4. Genotype vs Phenotype:
• Genotype – genetic makeup (AA, Aa, aa).
• Phenotype – physical expression (tall, short).

5. Homozygous vs Heterozygous:
• Homozygous – both alleles same (AA, aa).
• Heterozygous – different alleles (Aa).

6. Monohybrid and Dihybrid Cross (High School Important):
• Monohybrid – inheritance of one trait (3:1 ratio).
• Dihybrid – inheritance of two traits (9:3:3:1 ratio).

7. Sex Determination:
• Male gametes (sperm) determine the sex of offspring (X or Y).
• Female gametes (egg) only carry X chromosome.

8. Mutation (High School Important):
• Sudden change in DNA sequence leading to variation.
• Can be beneficial, harmful, or neutral.

9. Modern Genetics:
• DNA – carrier of genetic information.
• Genes code for proteins.
• Genetic engineering and biotechnology applications.
