Prepositions

Learn Prepositions and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence. They indicate direction, place, time, cause, manner, and possession.

Key Concepts :

1. Types of Prepositions:
• Prepositions of Place/Position – in, on, at, under, over
• Prepositions of Direction/Movement – to, into, onto, towards
• Prepositions of Time – at, on, in, since, for, during, by
• Prepositions of Cause/Reason/Purpose – because of, due to, for
• Prepositions of Instrument/Means/Agent – by, with, via
• Prepositions of Contrast/Concession – despite, in spite of
• Prepositions of Possession – of, to

2. Common Rules:
• "At" is used for specific time/place (at 5 pm, at school)
• "In" is used for months, years, centuries, long period (in 2020, in July)
• "On" is used for days and dates (on Monday, on 15th August)
• "Since" is used with the point of time; "For" is used with duration
• A preposition is always followed by a noun/pronoun (not by a verb)

3. Important Notes:
• "In" vs "Into" – "in" shows position, "into" shows movement
• "On" vs "Upon" – often interchangeable, "upon" is more formal
• Prepositions are never followed by a verb in base form, but by a noun/gerund (e.g., interested in reading)
