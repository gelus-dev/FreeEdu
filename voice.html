Voice

Learn Voice and Practice Questions ✅

Overview

Definition: Voice in grammar refers to the form of a verb that shows whether the subject of the sentence performs the action (Active Voice) or receives the action (Passive Voice).

Key Concepts :

1. Active Voice:
• The subject performs the action.
• Structure: Subject + Verb + Object
• Example: She writes a letter.

2. Passive Voice:
• The subject receives the action.
• Structure: Object of Active Sentence + Verb (to be + past participle) + by + Subject
• Example: A letter is written by her.

3. Conversion Rules:
• Tense must be maintained:
- Present Simple: writes → is written
- Past Simple: wrote → was written
- Present Continuous: is writing → is being written
- Past Continuous: was writing → was being written
- Present Perfect: has written → has been written
- Past Perfect: had written → had been written
- Future Simple: will write → will be written
- Future Perfect: will have written → will have been written
• Modal Verbs: can write → can be written, must write → must be written

4. Exceptions & Special Cases:
• Imperative sentences: “Open the door.” → “Let the door be opened.”
• Verbs without objects cannot form passive: “He sleeps.” (No passive form)
• Some active sentences can omit “by” if the agent is unknown or unimportant: “The book was written in 1990.” (agent not mentioned)
• Reflexive verbs usually stay active: “He washed himself.” → Passive is awkward.
• Infinitive forms: to write → to be written, having written → having been written

Practice Questions (MCQ)

Question 1:

Choose the correct passive form: “She writes a letter.”
A) A letter is written by her.
B) A letter was written by her.
C) A letter has been written by her.
D) A letter wrote by her.

Question 2:

Convert to passive: “They are painting the house.”
A) The house is painted by them.
B) The house is being painted by them.
C) The house was being painted by them.
D) The house has been painted by them.

Question 3:

Select the correct passive voice: “He will finish the work.”
A) The work will be finished by him.
B) The work will finished by him.
C) The work is finished by him.
D) The work has been finished by him.

Question 4:

Choose correct passive: “People speak English all over the world.”
A) English is spoken all over the world.
B) English spoke all over the world.
C) English was spoken all over the world.
D) English has spoken all over the world.

Question 5:

Convert to passive: “The teacher has corrected the papers.”
A) The papers has been corrected by the teacher.
B) The papers have been corrected by the teacher.
C) The papers were corrected by the teacher.
D) The papers is corrected by the teacher.

Question 6:

Select passive form: “He is writing a book.”
A) A book is written by him.
B) A book is being written by him.
C) A book was written by him.
D) A book has been written by him.

Question 7:

Choose the correct passive: “They will have completed the project.”
A) The project will have been completed by them.
B) The project will be completed by them.
C) The project has been completed by them.
D) The project was completed by them.

Question 8:

Convert to passive: “Open the door.”
A) The door is opened.
B) Let the door be opened.
C) The door was opened.
D) The door has opened.

Question 9:

Choose the correct passive voice: “He can solve the problem.”
A) The problem can be solved by him.
B) The problem can solve by him.
C) The problem is solved by him.
D) The problem has been solved by him.

Question 10:

Convert to passive: “She had written a letter.”
A) A letter had been written by her.
B) A letter has been written by her.
C) A letter was written by her.
D) A letter is written by her.

Answer Key

Answer 1:

A) A letter is written by her.

Answer 2:

B) The house is being painted by them.

Answer 3:

A) The work will be finished by him.

Answer 4:

Answer 5:

B) The papers have been corrected by the teacher.

Answer 6:
