Quadratic Equation

Learn about Quadratic Equation concepts, operations and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation of degree 2, generally written as \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \), where \( a \neq 0 \).

Key Formulas & Concepts:

1. Standard Form: \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \)

2. Factorization Method: Express as product of two linear factors: \( ax^2 + bx + c = (px + q)(rx + s) = 0 \)

3. Quadratic Formula: \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \)

4. Discriminant (Δ): \( \Delta = b^2 - 4ac \)
- Δ > 0: Two real and distinct roots
- Δ = 0: Two real and equal roots
- Δ < 0: Two complex roots

5. Sum & Product of Roots:
- Sum: \( \alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} \)
- Product: \( \alpha \beta = \frac{c}{a} \)

6. Nature of Roots: Based on Δ and coefficient signs, determine root types.

7. Vertex Form: \( y = a(x-h)^2 + k \), vertex at \( (h,k) = \left(-\frac{b}{2a}, -\frac{\Delta}{4a}\right) \)

Examples

Example 1: Factorization Method

Given: \( x^2 + 5x + 6 = 0 \)
Solution: Factorize: \( (x+2)(x+3)=0 \) → \( x=-2, -3 \)

Example 2: Using Quadratic Formula

Given: \( 2x^2 - 4x - 6 = 0 \)
Solution: \( x = \frac{-(-4) \pm \sqrt{(-4)^2 - 4*2*(-6)}}{2*2} = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16+48}}{4} = \frac{4 \pm 8}{4} \)
\( x = 3, -1 \)

Example 3: Nature of Roots

Given: \( x^2 + 4x + 4 = 0 \)
Solution: Δ = \( 16 -16 = 0 \) → Two real and equal roots: \( x = -2 \)

Example 4: Sum & Product of Roots

Given: \( 3x^2 - 5x + 2 = 0 \)
Solution: Sum = 5/3, Product = 2/3

Example 5: Vertex Form

Given: \( y = x^2 - 4x + 3 \)
Solution: Vertex at \( h = -(-4)/2 = 2, k = y(2) = 2^2 -4*2 +3 = -1 \) → Vertex = (2,-1)
