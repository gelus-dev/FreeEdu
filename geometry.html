Geometry

Learn about Geometry concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Geometry is the branch of mathematics that studies the sizes, shapes, properties, and dimensions of objects and spaces.

Basic Concepts:

1. Point: An exact location in space with no dimension.

2. Line: A straight one-dimensional figure extending infinitely in both directions.

3. Line Segment: A part of a line with two endpoints.

4. Ray: A part of a line with one endpoint extending infinitely in one direction.

5. Angle: Formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint.

Types of Angles:

• Acute: < 90°
• Right: = 90°
• Obtuse: > 90° and < 180°
• Straight: = 180°
• Reflex: > 180°

Important Formulas:

• Triangle Area (Heron’s formula): \( \Delta = \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}, s = \frac{a+b+c}{2} \)

• Triangle Area (base × height): \( \Delta = \frac{1}{2} \times base \times height \)

• Pythagoras Theorem: \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \) for right triangle

• Circle: Circumference \( C = 2\pi r \), Area \( A = \pi r^2 \)

• Quadrilateral: Sum of interior angles = 360°

• Polygon: Sum of interior angles = \( (n-2) \times 180° \), sum of exterior angles = 360°

• Volume of Sphere: \( V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \), Surface Area: \( 4\pi r^2 \)

• Volume of Cylinder: \( V = \pi r^2 h \), Surface Area: \( 2\pi r(h+r) \)

• Volume of Cone: \( V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h \), Surface Area: \( \pi r(l+r), l = \sqrt{r^2+h^2} \)

• Volume of Cuboid: \( V = l \cdot b \cdot h \), Surface Area: \( 2(lb+bh+hl) \)

• Volume of Cube: \( V = a^3 \), Surface Area: \( 6a^2 \)

Examples

Example 1: Triangle Area using Heron’s Formula

Given: Triangle with sides a=5, b=6, c=7
Find: Area
Solution: s=(5+6+7)/2=9; Area=√(9(9-5)(9-6)(9-7))=√216≈14.7

Example 2: Right Triangle using Pythagoras

Given: Right triangle, legs a=3, b=4
Find: Hypotenuse
Solution: c² = 3² + 4² = 9+16=25 → c=5

Example 3: Circle Area and Circumference

Given: r=7
Find: Area & Circumference
Solution: A = π*7² = 49π ≈ 153.94; C = 2π*7 = 14π ≈ 43.96
