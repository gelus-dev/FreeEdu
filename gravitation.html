Gravitation

Learn Gravitation concepts and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: Gravitation is the universal force of attraction that acts between all masses. It governs planetary motion, tides, and free-fall, and is described quantitatively by Newton’s Law of Gravitation.

Key Concepts:

1. Newton’s Law of Gravitation: The force between two masses \( m_1 \) and \( m_2 \) separated by distance \( r \) is:
\( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \)

2. Gravitational Constant: \( G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \mathrm{Nm^2/kg^2} \)

3. Acceleration due to Gravity: On Earth’s surface: \( g = \frac{GM}{R^2} \), where \( M \) and \( R \) are Earth’s mass and radius.

4. Weight: \( W = mg \)

5. Potential Energy: Gravitational potential energy near Earth: \( U = mgh \)
Universal form: \( U = - G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r} \)

6. Escape Velocity: \( v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}} \)

7. Orbital Velocity: \( v_o = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} \)

8. Kepler’s Laws:
• 1st Law: Orbit of planets is an ellipse with Sun at one focus.
• 2nd Law: Line joining planet and Sun sweeps equal areas in equal times.
• 3rd Law: \( \frac{T^2}{r^3} = \text{constant} \), \( T \) = orbital period, \( r \) = semi-major axis.

9. High School Level Formulas:
• Gravitational force: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \)
• g at height \( h \): \( g_h = g \left( 1 - \frac{2h}{R} \right) \) approx.
• g at depth \( d \): \( g_d = g \left( 1 - \frac{d}{R} \right) \) approx.
• Work done by gravity: \( W = \int F \, dr \)

Examples

Example 1: Gravitational Force

Given: \( m_1 = 5\,\mathrm{kg}, m_2 = 10\,\mathrm{kg}, r = 2\,\mathrm{m} \)
Find: Force between the masses.
Solution: \( F = G \frac{5*10}{2^2} = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} * \frac{50}{4} = 8.3425 \times 10^{-10}\, \mathrm{N} \)

Example 2: Acceleration due to Gravity at height

Given: \( h = 1000\,\mathrm{m} \), Earth radius \( R = 6.37 \times 10^6 \, \mathrm{m} \)
Find: \( g_h \)
Solution: \( g_h = g \left(1 - \frac{2h}{R}\right) = 9.8(1 - \frac{2000}{6.37*10^6}) \approx 9.7969\,\mathrm{m/s^2} \)

Example 3: Escape Velocity

Given: \( M = 5.972 \times 10^{24}\, \mathrm{kg}, R = 6.37 \times 10^6 \, \mathrm{m} \)
Find: \( v_e \)
Solution: \( v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}} = \sqrt{\frac{2*6.674e-11*5.972e24}{6.37e6}} \approx 11.2\,\mathrm{km/s} \)

Example 4: Orbital Velocity

Given: Satellite at \( r = 7 \times 10^6\,\mathrm{m} \) from Earth's center
Find: \( v_o \)
Solution: \( v_o = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} = \sqrt{\frac{6.674e-11 * 5.972e24}{7e6}} \approx 7.35\,\mathrm{km/s} \)
