Sequence and Series

Learn about sequences, series, types, formulas, and solved examples ✅

Overview

Definition: A sequence is an ordered list of numbers, while a series is the sum of the terms of a sequence.

Types of Sequences:

1. Arithmetic Sequence (A.P.): Sequence where the difference between consecutive terms is constant.
Example: 2, 5, 8, 11,... (common difference d = 3)

2. Geometric Sequence (G.P.): Sequence where the ratio of consecutive terms is constant.
Example: 3, 6, 12, 24,... (common ratio r = 2)

3. Harmonic Sequence: Sequence whose reciprocals form an arithmetic progression.
Example: 1, 1/2, 1/3, 1/4,...

Important Formulas:

• n-th term of A.P.: \( a_n = a + (n-1)d \)

• Sum of n terms of A.P.: \( S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n-1)d] \)

• n-th term of G.P.: \( a_n = ar^{n-1} \)

• Sum of n terms of G.P. (r ≠ 1): \( S_n = a\frac{1-r^n}{1-r} \)

• Sum of infinite G.P. (|r|<1): \( S_\infty = \frac{a}{1-r} \)

• Sum of first n natural numbers: \( S_n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \)

• Sum of squares of first n natural numbers: \( S_n = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} \)

• Sum of cubes of first n natural numbers: \( S_n = \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2 \)

Examples

Example 1: n-th term of A.P.

Given: A.P. 3, 7, 11,...
Find: 10th term
Solution: a = 3, d = 4; a₁₀ = 3 + (10-1)*4 = 3 + 36 = 39

Example 2: Sum of n terms of A.P.

Given: A.P. 2, 5, 8,..., n=20
Find: Sₙ
Solution: a = 2, d = 3, n=20; S₂₀ = 20/2[2*2 + 19*3] = 10[4 + 57] = 10*61 = 610

Example 3: Sum of n terms of G.P.

Given: G.P. 3, 6, 12,..., n=5
Find: S₅
Solution: a = 3, r = 2; S₅ = 3*(2⁵-1)/(2-1) = 3*(32-1)/1 = 3*31 = 93

Example 4: Infinite G.P.

Given: G.P. 8, 4, 2,...
Find: Sum to infinity
Solution: a = 8, r = 1/2; S∞ = 8/(1-1/2) = 8/(1/2) = 16

Example 5: Sum of squares

Find: Sum of squares of first 10 natural numbers
Solution: S = 10*11*21/6 = 385
